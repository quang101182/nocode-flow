n8n vs Zapier 2026: Which Automation Tool Actually Wins?

Introduction: Why This Comparison Matters

Workflow automation has gone from a nice-to-have to an absolute necessity for businesses of every size. Whether you are syncing leads from a form to your CRM, posting social media updates on a schedule, or orchestrating complex multi-step data pipelines, the tool you choose shapes how much time and money you save.

Two platforms dominate the conversation in 2026: n8n, the open-source, self-hostable powerhouse that has exploded in popularity among developers and technical teams, and Zapier, the market leader that pioneered the point-and-click automation space with more than six thousand integrations.

This guide breaks down every important dimension—features, pricing, ease of use, extensibility, and community support—so you can make an informed decision. We have used both platforms extensively and will share honest, experience-based insights rather than rehashing marketing pages.

What Is n8n?

n8n (pronounced "nodemation") is an open-source workflow automation platform created by Jan Oberhauser and released in 2019. It lets you connect apps, transform data, and build complex automations using a visual, node-based editor that runs in your browser.

Key Characteristics

  • Open Source: The source code is available on GitHub under the Sustainable Use License. You can inspect, modify, and extend it.
  • Self-Hosted Option: Run n8n on your own server—a $5/month VPS, a Raspberry Pi, or a Kubernetes cluster. Your data never leaves your infrastructure.
  • 400+ Built-In Integrations: From Google Sheets and Slack to Postgres, MQTT, and custom HTTP requests. Community nodes push this number even higher.
  • Code When You Need It: Every workflow can include JavaScript or Python code nodes, giving you escape hatches when visual logic is not enough.
  • No Per-Execution Pricing: When self-hosted, there are zero usage limits. Run a million workflows a day if your server can handle it.
  • Sub-Workflows: Call one workflow from another, enabling modular, reusable automation architectures.
  • Version Control Friendly: Workflows are stored as JSON, making it easy to track changes in Git.

Who Uses n8n?

n8n has found a strong following among startups, DevOps teams, data engineers, and privacy-conscious organizations. Companies like Hewlett Packard Enterprise, Sennheiser, and dozens of Y Combinator startups rely on n8n in production. The platform raised $12 million in Series A funding and continues to grow its team and feature set rapidly.

What Is Zapier?

Zapier is the most widely recognized cloud automation platform in the world. Founded in 2011 by Wade Foster, Bryan Helmig, and Mike Knoop, it has grown into a company valued at over $5 billion with more than 700 employees—all operating as a fully remote team.

Key Characteristics

  • 6,000+ App Integrations: The largest integration library in the automation space, covering everything from niche CRMs to enterprise ERPs.
  • Cloud-Only: Zapier runs entirely on its own infrastructure. There is no self-hosted option.
  • Zap Editor: The workflow builder uses a step-by-step linear editor. Recent updates have added branching paths and filters, but the paradigm remains sequential.
  • Zapier Tables: A built-in database for storing and querying automation data without needing external tools.
  • Zapier Interfaces: Build simple web forms and pages that trigger Zaps, useful for internal tools.
  • AI Actions: Native integration with OpenAI, Anthropic, and other LLMs directly in your Zaps.
  • Transfer Tool: Bulk data migration between apps without building a workflow.

Who Uses Zapier?

Zapier's user base skews toward non-technical professionals, marketers, operations managers, and small business owners who need automation without writing code. However, larger companies also use it for departmental automations. Zapier reports that its platform runs over two billion tasks per year across more than 600,000 businesses.

Pricing Comparison (2026)

Pricing is often the deciding factor when choosing between these two platforms. Here is a clear breakdown of what each will cost you.

n8n Pricing

  • Self-Hosted (Free): Unlimited workflows, unlimited executions. You only pay for your server (as low as $5/month on providers like Hetzner or DigitalOcean).
  • n8n Cloud Starter ($20/month): 2,500 workflow executions, 5 active workflows, community support.
  • n8n Cloud Pro ($50/month): 10,000 executions, 15 active workflows, execution log, global variables.
  • n8n Cloud Enterprise (Custom): Unlimited executions, SSO, LDAP, dedicated support, SLA guarantees.

Zapier Pricing

  • Free: 100 tasks/month, 5 Zaps, single-step only.
  • Starter ($19.99/month): 750 tasks/month, 20 Zaps, multi-step workflows, filters.
  • Professional ($49/month): 2,000 tasks/month, unlimited Zaps, paths (branching), webhooks, custom logic.
  • Team ($69.50/month): 2,000 tasks/month, unlimited users, shared app connections, premier support.
  • Enterprise (Custom): Advanced admin, SSO, SCIM, dedicated account manager.

The Real Cost Difference

The biggest cost difference becomes apparent at scale. A self-hosted n8n instance running 50,000 executions per month costs you roughly $10 to $20 in server expenses. The equivalent volume on Zapier's Professional plan would require purchasing additional task packs, easily reaching $150 to $300 per month or more depending on the number of steps per Zap.

However, self-hosting n8n has hidden costs: server maintenance, updates, backups, and the technical knowledge to manage it all. If your team lacks DevOps skills, n8n Cloud or Zapier may be more cost-effective when you account for time spent on infrastructure.

Pros and Cons

n8n Pros

  • Completely free when self-hosted with no execution limits
  • Full data ownership and privacy compliance (GDPR, HIPAA-friendly)
  • Powerful visual editor with advanced logic (loops, branching, merge)
  • JavaScript and Python code nodes for unlimited customization
  • Thriving open-source community with 38,000+ GitHub stars
  • Built-in AI and LangChain support for agent workflows
  • Workflows exportable as JSON for version control

n8n Cons

  • Smaller integration library (400+ vs 6,000+)
  • Self-hosting requires technical knowledge (Docker, Linux basics)
  • Steeper learning curve for non-technical users
  • Cloud plans are relatively new and less polished than Zapier's
  • Documentation, while improving, can be sparse for advanced use cases

Zapier Pros

  • Largest integration library in the industry (6,000+ apps)
  • Extremely beginner-friendly with guided setup wizards
  • Zero infrastructure to manage—everything runs in the cloud
  • Reliable uptime with enterprise-grade infrastructure
  • Excellent documentation, templates, and Zapier University courses
  • Zapier Tables and Interfaces add no-code database and form capabilities
  • Strong brand trust and proven track record since 2011

Zapier Cons

  • Task-based pricing gets expensive quickly at scale
  • No self-hosting option—data always passes through Zapier servers
  • Limited branching and no native loop support
  • Code capabilities are more restricted than n8n's
  • Webhooks and premium features locked behind higher plans
  • Multi-step Zaps consume multiple tasks per execution

When to Choose n8n vs Zapier

Choose n8n If You...

  • Need cost-effective scaling: Your automation volume is growing and you want predictable costs. Self-hosted n8n with 100K+ executions per month costs a fraction of Zapier.
  • Require data privacy: You handle sensitive customer data, medical records, or financial information and need to keep everything on your own servers.
  • Build complex workflows: Your automations involve loops, conditional branches, error handling with retry logic, and data transformations that go beyond simple A-to-B connections.
  • Have technical skills: You or your team are comfortable with Docker, basic server administration, and occasionally writing JavaScript or Python.
  • Want AI agent workflows: n8n's native LangChain integration and AI nodes make it an excellent platform for building autonomous AI agents and RAG pipelines.

Choose Zapier If You...

  • Value simplicity above all: You want to connect two apps in under five minutes without touching any technical configuration.
  • Need a specific integration: The app you need to connect is only available on Zapier's 6,000+ library and does not have a public API you can use via n8n's HTTP node.
  • Have a non-technical team: Your marketing, sales, or operations team needs to build and maintain automations without developer support.
  • Prefer zero maintenance: You do not want to manage servers, updates, SSL certificates, or database backups.
  • Run low-volume workflows: If you execute fewer than 750 tasks per month, Zapier's Starter plan at $19.99 is a solid value.

The Verdict

There is no single winner because these tools serve different audiences. n8n is the better choice for technical teams, developers, and anyone who needs powerful automation at scale without breaking the bank. Its open-source nature, self-hosting capability, and advanced workflow features make it unbeatable for complex use cases.

Zapier is the better choice for non-technical users who need quick, reliable automations with the widest possible app coverage. Its polished interface, massive template library, and zero-maintenance approach make it the gold standard for business users.

Our recommendation: if you have even basic technical skills, start with n8n's self-hosted version. You get unlimited power at zero cost. If you are a non-technical professional who needs to automate today, Zapier will get you productive in minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is n8n really free?

Yes. n8n is open-source under the Sustainable Use License. You can self-host it on your own server at no cost with unlimited workflows and executions. n8n also offers a managed cloud plan starting at $20 per month if you prefer not to manage infrastructure.

Can Zapier do everything n8n can?

Zapier has a larger app library with over 6,000 integrations compared to n8n's 400+. However, n8n offers more advanced features like conditional branching, looping, custom code nodes, self-hosting, and no per-task pricing. For complex, developer-oriented workflows n8n is often more capable.

Which is better for beginners?

Zapier is generally easier for complete beginners thanks to its polished interface, extensive templates, and guided setup. n8n has a steeper initial learning curve, especially if you self-host, but its visual node editor is intuitive once you get started.

Can I migrate my Zapier workflows to n8n?

There is no one-click migration tool, but n8n supports most of the same services Zapier does. You would need to rebuild your Zaps as n8n workflows manually. The n8n community provides templates that replicate common Zapier automations, making the transition easier.
