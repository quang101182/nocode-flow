The Real Cost of Automation: It Is Not What You Think

Zapier's pricing page says "starts at $19.99/month." n8n says "free and open-source." But what does automation actually cost when you factor in task limits, premium apps, overage charges, hosting costs, and the time you spend managing it all?

We ran the numbers for four real-world scenarios — freelancer, startup, small-to-medium business, and enterprise. The results surprised us. In some cases, Zapier costs 50x more than n8n for the same number of automations.

This is not a feature comparison (we have a full n8n vs Zapier comparison for that). This article is purely about money: what you will pay, where the hidden costs are, and which tool makes financial sense for your situation.

Zapier Pricing Breakdown (2026)

Zapier uses a task-based pricing model. Every action in a workflow counts as one "task." A 5-step Zap that runs once uses 5 tasks. Run it 100 times and that is 500 tasks. This adds up fast.

Zapier Plans and Prices

Plan Monthly Price Annual Price Tasks/Month Cost per 1K Tasks
Free $0 $0 100 $0 (very limited)
Starter $19.99 $239.88 750 $26.65
Professional $49 $588 2,000 $24.50
Team $69 $828 2,000 $34.50
Enterprise Custom Custom Custom Negotiable

Zapier's Hidden Costs

  • Task overage: Exceed your plan's task limit and Zapier automatically bumps you to the next tier. No warning, just a higher bill
  • Premium apps: Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics, and other "premium" integrations require the Professional plan ($49/month) minimum. You cannot use them on Starter
  • Multi-step Zaps: The free tier only supports single-step Zaps (1 trigger + 1 action). Any real workflow requires a paid plan
  • Filters count as tasks: Even filter steps that do not produce output count toward your task limit
  • Paths (branching): Each branch in a Path step consumes tasks independently, even if only one branch executes
  • Update frequency: Free and Starter plans check triggers every 15 minutes. Professional checks every 2 minutes. Want real-time? That is Team tier ($69/month)

Real-World Zapier Cost Example

Imagine a simple workflow: when a form is submitted, create a CRM contact, send a welcome email, add to a Google Sheet, and notify Slack. That is 4 tasks per submission. If you get 50 form submissions per day:

  • 50 submissions × 4 tasks = 200 tasks/day
  • 200 tasks × 30 days = 6,000 tasks/month
  • Professional plan (2,000 tasks) is not enough → need to purchase additional task packs
  • Real cost: approximately $99-149/month ($1,188-1,788/year)

n8n's Cost Advantages

  • Execution-based, not task-based: A 10-node workflow that runs once = 1 execution. On Zapier, that same workflow = 10 tasks. n8n is immediately 10x cheaper by this metric alone
  • No premium app surcharges: Every integration works on every plan. Salesforce, HubSpot, and all other nodes are available even on the free tier
  • No overage surprises: Self-hosted has no limits. Cloud plans show clear execution counts with no automatic tier upgrades
  • AI nodes included: OpenAI, Claude, and other AI integrations do not cost extra. On Zapier, AI features require Professional tier minimum

Self-Hosting n8n: The Complete Cost Picture

Self-hosting is n8n's biggest pricing advantage, but it is not truly "free." Here is the honest breakdown of total costs:

Direct Costs

Item Monthly Cost Notes

Domain (optional) $1 ~$12/year for a .com domain
SSL certificate $0 Free with Let's Encrypt / Caddy
Backups $1-2 Automated VPS snapshots
Total $7-9/month For unlimited workflows and executions

Indirect Costs (Time)

  • Initial setup: 1-2 hours (Docker install, configure reverse proxy, set up backups)
  • Monthly maintenance: 30 minutes (update n8n, check logs, verify backups)
  • Troubleshooting: 0-2 hours/month (depends on complexity of your workflows)

At $40/hour, the time cost is roughly $20-80/month in the first month, then $20-60/month ongoing. Even with this factored in, self-hosted n8n is still cheaper than Zapier for any non-trivial usage.

When Self-Hosting Does NOT Make Sense

  • You have zero technical experience and no one on your team does either
  • You need guaranteed uptime SLAs for mission-critical workflows
  • Your company requires vendor-managed security certifications (SOC 2, HIPAA)
  • You only need 2-3 simple workflows (n8n Cloud free tier or Zapier free tier is enough)

When Zapier Is Still Worth the Price

Despite the cost difference, Zapier has legitimate advantages that may justify the price for some users:

  • 7,000+ native integrations: If you use niche tools that n8n does not support natively, Zapier might connect to them out of the box. n8n's HTTP Request node can connect to any API, but it requires more setup
  • Zero technical barrier: Zapier is genuinely the easiest automation tool to learn. If your team is non-technical and time-to-value matters more than cost, Zapier's simplicity has real value
  • Guaranteed reliability: Zapier's infrastructure is battle-tested at scale. Self-hosted n8n requires you to manage uptime yourself
  • Built-in AI actions: Zapier's AI features (like AI-powered text formatting) are integrated directly into the platform, which is convenient if you do not need advanced AI workflows
  • Team collaboration: Zapier's Team plan includes shared workspaces, permissions, and activity logs that are more polished than n8n Cloud's current offering

Bottom line: If you run fewer than 500 tasks/month and value simplicity above all, Zapier's Starter plan at $19.99/month is reasonable. Beyond that threshold, n8n delivers dramatically more value per dollar.

Our Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

Choose n8n Self-Hosted If:

  • You or someone on your team can run a Docker container (it takes 10 minutes to learn)
  • You want unlimited workflows and executions for under $10/month
  • Data privacy matters (your data stays on your server)
  • You need advanced features like AI nodes, custom code, or complex branching

Choose n8n Cloud If:

  • You want the power of n8n without managing a server
  • You need a managed solution with automatic updates
  • Your execution count is under 10,000/month

Choose Zapier If:

  • Your team is completely non-technical
  • You need very specific niche integrations that only Zapier supports
  • You run fewer than 500 tasks/month and simplicity is your top priority

For the vast majority of users — freelancers, startups, and SMBs — n8n is the clear winner on pricing. The cost savings compound year after year, and the platform's capabilities are on par with (or exceed) Zapier's.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is n8n really free?

Yes, n8n is open-source and completely free when self-hosted. You can run unlimited workflows with unlimited executions. The only cost is the server — as low as $5/month on a VPS or $0 on your own hardware. n8n Cloud has a free tier (5 workflows, 2,500 executions/month) and paid plans starting at $20/month.

How much does Zapier cost per month in 2026?

Zapier's pricing starts with a limited free tier (100 tasks/month, 5 Zaps). Paid plans: Starter at $19.99/month (750 tasks), Professional at $49/month (2,000 tasks), Team at $69/month (2,000 tasks with collaboration). Enterprise pricing is custom. These are annual billing prices.

What is cheaper: Zapier or n8n?

n8n is significantly cheaper in almost every scenario. Self-hosted n8n costs $0-7/month for unlimited usage. Even n8n Cloud at $20/month includes far more value than Zapier's $49/month Professional plan. For businesses running 10,000+ tasks/month, n8n can be 80-95% cheaper.

How much does it cost to self-host n8n?

Self-hosting n8n costs between $0 and $20/month. Running on your own hardware or a Raspberry Pi costs $0. A basic VPS (Hetzner, DigitalOcean) costs $5-12/month. The n8n software itself is always free with unlimited everything.

Does Zapier have hidden costs?

Yes. Each action step counts as a task (a 5-step Zap = 5 tasks per run). Premium apps require higher-tier plans. Exceeding your task limit triggers automatic tier upgrades. Filters and paths consume tasks even when they do not produce output. Real-time triggers require the Team plan ($69/month).
